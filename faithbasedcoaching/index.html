About Ethan

Faith is the foundation, not a footnote.

I’ve spent 12+ years helping people figure out their next career move. The common thread across this work is that I’m gifted at taking something messy and making it clear. I see my faith as the foundation of how I help others in this way. I’ve spent years having conversations at the intersection of faith, identity, and calling. That work convinced me of something I still believe: too many Christians are living below what God has for them—not because they don’t care, but because no one has helped them connect the deeper parts of who they are to what they actually do.

I believe God uses coaching as a tool for alignment. He’s given each of us a distinct wiring, a book he’s still writing, and career transitions are often where that story takes a significant turn. If you’re navigating a job search, a career change, or a creeping sense that your work doesn’t fit who you’re becoming, I’d love to help you think through it—practically and holistically.

A bit more about my background:

  • › I’ve spent much of my adult life serving in churches—designing spiritually-oriented curriculum, leading spiritual formation experiences, facilitating small groups, speaking in classes and at retreats, and preaching to large groups.
  • › B.A. in Biblical Studies and History from John Brown University · M.A. in Christian Formation from Wheaton College.
  • › I’m grateful to have experienced transformational travel to Europe, studying and walking the historical roots of the Christian faith firsthand in Italy and Germany.
